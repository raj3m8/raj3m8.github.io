Frequently Asked Questions

What makes vLoop unique?

We pride ourselves on being the easiest platform to use at the best price. Our mission is to make video available to everyone. vLoop provides professional level functionality at a fraction of the cost. We are major innovators in the industry in terms of product design. We solve the challenge of combining communication and collaboration with video in a way that benefits everyone.

Can I import videos from Dropbox?

Yes of course! You can import multiple videos at once too. You can also import videos instantly from other vLoop users with our 'vLoop Share URLs' which makes it very convenient for leagues or conferences using vLoop.

What kind of technology goes into making a platform like this?

Building vLoop is an ongoing project that requires a multi discpinlary team. Working with large video files requires serious backend processing power. We have a team of people dedicated specifically to that part of the platform. Our user experience team is comprised of front-end web developers, data scientists and UX desginers to ensure that we are using the most up to date web technologies to maximize platform performance and optimize our user experience through intuitive work flows. We also have a number of professional athletes and former college athletes on our team so we cna make sure we are building the right things for our customers.

Can my players create their own highlight reels?

Absolutely! This is one of our favorite parts of the platform. We know how much of a pain in the a$$ and how expensive creating highlight reels can be. All vLoop users have the ability to create as many highlight reels as they want at no additional cost.

Is video analysis the only reason to use vLoop?

Video analysis is definitely a great reason to use vLoop, but it is far from the only one. Here are a few others that we've seen: 1) For parents who want to create and share clips of their kids. 2) An easy private cloud storage platform to give multiple people access to upload and view video files. 3) For players to have fun and chat about their games. 4) If you just want to clip and draw on videos.

Can I use video with young players?

Definitely. It can be a positive and engaging way to teach them. The reality is they are probably more comfortable with technology than you are. We have seen a lot of great success stories even down to the U10 level. The key to using video at the younger ages seems to be keeping things simple and also focusing on letting the kids use the tools for themselves. Its a great way to get parents involved in a positve capaicty as well.
